I'm Aidan, an Electrical Engineering and Computer Science student at UC Berkeley (graduating May 2027) with a focus on analog, mixed-signal, and low-power electronics. I design circuits and PCBs, write embedded firmware, and lead the STAR avionics team.

I'm the Avionics Lead for Space Technologies and Rocketry (STAR), the competitive rocketry team at Berkeley. I lead 10+ engineers building flight computers, data acquisition systems, and payload avionics. I've designed PCBs for flight telemetry, analog data acquisition, motor control, valve actuation, and power distribution. Previously, as Vice President, I built an education program that had new members constructing four high-power rockets in their first semester.

At Berkeley, I've taken graduate-level analog IC design courses (EE 240B, EE 240C), noise analysis, FinFet design, transistor level design of ADCand ADC topologies in Cadence Virtuoso. I was a finalist in the EE 140 (analog integrated circuits) design competition for an ultra-low-power smartwatch display driver. I also direct Advanced PCB Engineering (APE), a DeCal course through UC Berkeley IEEE, where I teach 20+ students each semester in topics such as high-speed digital, precision analog, power electronics, and RF layout.

I've interned twice at Astranis Space Technologies, where I designed and brought up avionics PCBs for spacecraft integration, including boards that emulate propulsion systems, monitor spacecraft health, and automate tedious checkouts. I also worked launch operations at Cape Canaveral, running electrical checkouts and creating the systen that last charged the Block 2 satellites before they launched!

My toolbox spans Altium and KiCad for PCB design, Cadence Virtuoso and Spectre for IC simulation, C/C++ and Python for firmware and test infrastructure, Verilog for digital design, and SolidWorks, OnShape, and Fusion 360 for MCAD. I'm also an Eagle Scout, from Troop 726.
